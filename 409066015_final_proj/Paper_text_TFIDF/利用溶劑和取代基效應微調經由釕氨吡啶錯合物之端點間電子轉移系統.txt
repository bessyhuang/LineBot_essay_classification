利用溶劑和取代基效應微調經由釕氨吡啶錯合物之端點間電子轉移系統
Tuning End-to-End Electron Transfer System Through Rutheniumam(m)ine -Pyridine Moiety Via Solvent and Substituent Effect
電子轉移
本篇論文乃針對七個以二茂鐵吡啶及釕氨為主體之單核、雙核及三核金屬cis,trans-[Ru(NH3)4(R1)2]2+ 3+(R1= phpy )，[Ru(NH3)4(R2-Fcpy) -(py)]2+ 3+ (R2=Et-,H-) ，和cis-,trans-[Ru(NH3)4(R2-Fcpy)2]2+ 3+ 4+(R2= Et-,H-)混價錯合物的合成方法及特性，並且試著去解釋含有取代基Et-三核金屬混價錯合物端點間電子轉移的現象。利用光激發電子轉移的能量(Eop)與FΔE1 2(ΔE1 2=E1 2(FeIII II)－E1 2(RuIII II))線性迴歸作圖得到雙核和三核金屬錯合物的基態重組能，結果顯示位能面的變形(distortion)，三核大於雙核。在含有取代基Et-三核金屬混價錯合物中，成功地利用溶劑的調控(在R-C≡N系列溶劑)使得端點鐵和鐵之間的電子耦合增強，使原本鐵為一次兩個電子的氧化還原波會分裂開來成為二次各一個電子的氧化還原波。我們利用在R-C≡N系列溶劑的電化學數據去計算含有取代基Et-三核金屬混價錯合物，端點間的電子耦合參數Hab(end-to-end)，所得到的數值較中間橋基含有兩個苯的雙二茂鐵( [Fc-ph-ph-Fc]0 1+)端點間的電子耦合參數Hab(end-to-end)來得大。
The thesis is focused on the synthesis and characterization of seven mono- , dimmer- and trimetal mixed-valence complexes including ferrocenylpyridine and rutheniumammine moieties, cis-,trans-[Ru(NH3)4 -(R1)2]2+ 3+(R1= phpy ),[Ru(NH3)4(R2-Fcpy)(py)]2+ 3+(R2=Et-,H-) and cis-,trans-[Ru(NH3)4(R2-Fcpy)2]2+ 3+ 4+(R2= Et-,H-) and tried to explain the behavior of end-to-end electron transfer of cis-,trans-[Ru(NH3)4 (R2-Fcpy)2]4+(R2= Et-,H-).The linear regression for opitical electron transfer energy (Eop) vs F△E1 2(ΔE1 2=E1 2(FeIII II)－E1 2(RuIII II)) resulted the reorganizational energy of mixed-valence complexes and imply that the ground state distortional of cis-,trans-[Ru(NH3)4(R2-Fcpy)2]3+ (R2= Et-,H-) than of [Ru(NH3)4(R2-Fcpy)(py)]3+(R2=Et-,H-). For cis-,trans-[Ru(NH3)4 -(R2-Fcpy)2]2+ 3+ 4+ (R2= Et-), the two electrochemical redox curves are an electron (RuIII II) redox process  and two electron (FeIII II) redox process in high donor-number solvent but R-C≡N solvent (three one-electron redox process).We calculate the electronic coupling Hab(end-to-end) with the electrochemistry data of cis-,trans-[Ru(NH3)4(R2-Fcpy)2]2+ 3+ 4+ (R2= Et-) in R-C≡N solvent and the Hab(end-to-end) , is much large than the electronic coupling Hab(end-to-end) of the classical Fc-bridge-Fc complexes, ( bridge =4,4’-biphenyl).
